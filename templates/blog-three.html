How To get good tenders and bids

There are several steps that suppliers or contractors can take to increase their chances of getting good tenders and bids:

Research: Understand the organization and the project by researching the organization's needs and priorities, and by reading the tender document carefully. This will help you to tailor your proposal to the specific requirements of the organization.

Network: Build relationships with organizations that issue tenders by attending industry events, joining trade associations, and networking with other suppliers or contractors.

Compliance: Ensure that your proposal complies with all relevant laws and regulations related to tenders, such as public procurement laws.

Tailor your proposal: Tailor your proposal to the specific requirements of the organization and project by highlighting your relevant experience and qualifications.

Cost estimates: Provide detailed cost estimates that are accurate and realistic.

Quality assurance: Emphasize your commitment to quality assurance by outlining your processes and procedures for ensuring that goods or services are delivered to the required standard.

Innovation: Highlight any innovative solutions or new products or services that you can offer to the organization.

Marketing: Use marketing strategies to promote your company and its capabilities to potential clients, specially the ones that are likely to issue tenders.

Be responsive: Be responsive to any questions or clarifications that the organization may have about your proposal.

Follow-up: Follow up with the organization after the submission deadline to inquire about the status of your proposal and to express your continued interest in the project.

By taking these steps, suppliers and contractors can increase their chances of getting good tenders and bids. It's also important to mention that having a good reputation and experience in the market could be a valuable asset to win a tender.
